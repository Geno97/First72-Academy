Chainsaw Safety & Operation

An awareness-level introduction to chainsaw risks and safe practice. This course does NOT qualify you to operate a chainsaw — accredited in-person training is required.

01

Chainsaw safety awareness

Understanding the risks before you start.

 OPEN / CLOSE

Chainsaws are one of the most dangerous tools in emergency clean-up. This course raises awareness of the risks — it does NOT qualify you to operate one.

STOP — read this first

Hands-on training required

You must NOT operate a chainsaw based on this course. Chainsaw operation requires accredited, in-person training with a qualified instructor, the right protective equipment, and supervised practice. This course is awareness only.

A chainsaw can cause life-changing injury in a fraction of a second. Never operate one without proper training.
02

Protective equipment

What proper protection looks like.

 OPEN / CLOSE

Trained operators always wear full personal protective equipment (PPE). Understanding this helps you recognise safe practice.

  • Chainsaw-rated trousers or chaps.
  • Helmet with face and hearing protection.
  • Cut-resistant gloves and sturdy boots.
  • Never operate alone; never when tired or rushed.
03

Recognising hazards

Why clean-up cutting is so risky.

 OPEN / CLOSE

Storm-damaged trees are especially dangerous — under tension, unstable, and unpredictable. Even experienced operators treat them with great caution.

  • Tension and compression in fallen trees can cause violent movement.
  • Power lines may be tangled in fallen branches — treat as live.
  • Unstable ground and debris add risk.
  • If in doubt, leave it to a professional arborist.
Storm-damaged trees are a job for trained professionals. When in doubt, stay out.
