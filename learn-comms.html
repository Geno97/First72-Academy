Emergency Communications

An awareness-level introduction to emergency communications and radio use. Accredited training is required for certified roles.

Draft content. This course is a practical starting point. Please review it and have someone with relevant expertise check anything safety-critical before relying on it.

01

Why communications matter

Keeping information flowing when it counts.

 OPEN / CLOSE

In an emergency, clear communication keeps people safe and coordinated. When normal networks fail, radios and disciplined messaging become essential.

Important

This is awareness-level content only

This course gives general awareness, not certification. For a recognised qualification or any hands-on practice, you must complete accredited training with a qualified instructor and the official course materials. Do not rely on this course alone for safety-critical or certified work.

Clear, calm, disciplined messages save time and prevent mistakes.
02

Radio basics

Using two-way radio clearly.

 OPEN / CLOSE

Two-way radios work when phones don't. Using them well means being brief, clear, and following a shared discipline. (Specific procedures and call signs are covered in accredited training.)

  • Listen before you transmit.
  • Keep messages short and clear.
  • Speak slowly and plainly; confirm important information.
  • Use plain language unless trained otherwise.
03

Message discipline

Getting the right message through.

 OPEN / CLOSE

Good message discipline means others get accurate information fast.

  • Say what's confirmed; flag what isn't.
  • Prioritise urgent safety information.
  • Keep a log of important messages in and out.
  • Pass serious needs to the ECC/EOC.
Discipline on the radio means everyone gets the right information, fast.
