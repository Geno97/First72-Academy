Property Damage Mitigation & Storm Response

Limit storm damage to property — preparing before bad weather, staying safe during it, and cleaning up safely afterward.

Draft content. This course is a practical starting point. Please review it and have someone with relevant expertise check anything safety-critical before relying on it.

Click any module to expand it. Each one has a short lesson, and most include an activity or a quick knowledge check. Work at your own pace.

01

Before the storm

Preparation that limits damage.

 OPEN / CLOSE

Most storm damage can be reduced with preparation. A bit of work before bad weather saves a lot of damage after.

  • Secure or store loose outdoor items.
  • Clear gutters and drains so water flows away.
  • Trim branches near buildings and power lines (safely, or get a professional).
  • Check the roof and windows for weak points.
An hour of preparation beats a week of repairs.
02

During and after

Staying safe and limiting further damage.

 OPEN / CLOSE

During a storm, stay inside and away from windows. Afterwards, make the property safe before assessing damage.

  • Stay clear of windows and damaged areas during the storm.
  • After it passes, watch for hazards — downed lines, broken glass, unstable structures.
  • Make temporary repairs to stop water getting in (tarpaulins, boarding).
  • Photograph damage for insurance before you clean up.
Activity

Make a storm kit

List what you'd need to secure your property fast — tarps, rope, torch, tools — and where it's kept.

03

Debris and clean-up

Clearing safely after the event.

 OPEN / CLOSE

Clean-up is where many post-storm injuries happen. Treat downed lines as live, and don't take on jobs beyond your skill.

  • Treat every downed power line as live and dangerous — stay well back.
  • Wear gloves and sturdy footwear.
  • Lift safely and don't overdo it.
  • Leave dangerous trees and structures to professionals.
Treat every downed power line as live. Assume it can kill, and keep everyone away.
