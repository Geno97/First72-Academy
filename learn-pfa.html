Psychological First Aid

Offer calm, practical support to people in distress after a crisis — using the look, listen, link approach anyone can learn.

01

What psychological first aid is

Supporting people in distress.

 OPEN / CLOSE

Psychological First Aid (PFA) is a way of offering humane, practical support to people in distress after a crisis. It's not therapy — it's calm, caring human support that anyone can learn.

You don't need to be a counsellor to make someone feel safer and less alone.
02

Look, listen, link

A simple, caring approach.

 OPEN / CLOSE

A widely used PFA approach is to look, listen, and link.

  • LOOK — notice who needs support and whether they're safe.
  • LISTEN — be present, listen without pushing, and let people share at their pace.
  • LINK — connect people to information, practical help, and ongoing support.
Activity

Practise presence

Think about a time someone simply listened to you well. What did they do? Those small behaviours are the heart of PFA.

03

Do and avoid

Helpful and unhelpful responses.

 OPEN / CLOSE

Small things make a big difference when someone is distressed.

Do

  • Stay calm and speak gently.
  • Respect people's choices and dignity.
  • Offer practical help — water, a quiet space, information.
  • Know your limits and refer on when needed.

Avoid

  • Pressuring people to talk.
  • Minimising their feelings or rushing them.
  • Making promises you can't keep.
  • Sharing their private information.
If someone is at risk of harm to themselves or others, connect them with professional help immediately.
