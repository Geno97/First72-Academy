Spontaneous Volunteer Management

Register, match, and look after the volunteers who arrive to help in an emergency — turning goodwill into organised, safe support.

Draft content. This course is a practical starting point. Please review it and have someone with relevant expertise check anything safety-critical before relying on it.

Click any module to expand it. Each one has a short lesson, and most include an activity or a quick knowledge check. Work at your own pace.

01

Why volunteer management matters

Goodwill needs structure to be useful.

 OPEN / CLOSE

After a disaster, people show up wanting to help. Unmanaged, that goodwill can create confusion and risk. Managed well, it's one of a community's greatest strengths.

An organised volunteer is an asset. An unmanaged one can become a liability.
02

Registering volunteers

A simple sign-in that captures what you need.

 OPEN / CLOSE

Register everyone who offers to help. Keep it quick but capture the essentials.

  • Name and contact details.
  • Skills, training, or useful equipment.
  • Availability and any limitations.
  • A simple sign-in / sign-out so you know who's on site.
Activity

Design a sign-in

Draft a one-page volunteer sign-in sheet capturing name, contact, skills, and availability.

03

Matching people to tasks

Right person, right job, safely.

 OPEN / CLOSE

Match volunteers to tasks that suit their skills and keep them safe. Never send people into situations beyond their training.

  • Match skills to needs where you can.
  • Brief people clearly before they start.
  • Pair newcomers with experienced volunteers.
  • Never assign tasks that exceed someone's training or safety.
Facilitate and direct — don't just let willing people wander into risk.
04

Wellbeing and stand-down

Looking after volunteers, and closing out.

 OPEN / CLOSE

Volunteers tire and feel stress too. Rotate them, support them, and sign them out properly when they leave.

  • Prompt breaks, food, water, and rest.
  • Watch for fatigue and stress; rotate roles.
  • Thank people — recognition matters.
  • Sign volunteers out so you always know who's on site.
