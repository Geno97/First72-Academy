Community Evacuation Assistance

Help your community evacuate safely — supporting vulnerable people and coordinating calm, safe movement to safety.

Draft content. This course is a practical starting point. Please review it and have someone with relevant expertise check anything safety-critical before relying on it.

Click any module to expand it. Each one has a short lesson, and most include an activity or a quick knowledge check. Work at your own pace.

01

Understanding evacuation

When and why communities move.

 OPEN / CLOSE

Evacuation moves people from danger to safety. Community members can play a vital role in helping neighbours move calmly and safely — especially those who need extra help.

A calm, supported evacuation saves lives. Panic costs them.
02

Helping vulnerable people

Those who need extra support to move.

 OPEN / CLOSE

Some people can't evacuate easily on their own — older people, those with disabilities, families with young children, or anyone without transport.

  • Know who in your area may need help in advance, where possible.
  • Offer help respectfully — let people keep control of their decisions.
  • Assist with mobility, transport, and carrying essentials.
  • Never force someone; report those who refuse or can't be reached.
Activity

Map your neighbours

Think about who near you might need help to evacuate. How would you check on them quickly?

03

Safe movement

Getting people out without creating new risks.

 OPEN / CLOSE

Move people by safe routes, keep groups together, and don't put yourself or others in danger.

  • Use routes confirmed as safe; avoid floodwater and damaged structures.
  • Keep groups together and account for everyone.
  • Carry only essentials — don't delay for belongings.
  • Direct people to the designated safe location or assembly point.
Never enter floodwater or unstable buildings to assist — you can't help anyone if you become a casualty.
