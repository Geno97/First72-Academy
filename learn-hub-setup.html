Community Hub Setup & Response

Establish and run a community-led emergency hub — choosing a venue, opening in an orderly way, and keeping it functioning.

Draft content. This course is a practical starting point. Please review it and have someone with relevant expertise check anything safety-critical before relying on it.

Click any module to expand it. Each one has a short lesson, and most include an activity or a quick knowledge check. Work at your own pace.

01

What a community hub is for

A local place to gather, inform, and solve problems.

 OPEN / CLOSE

A Community Emergency Hub is a place run by locals where a community can gather safely, share information, and solve local problems using local resources. It's not a rescue centre — it's a coordination point.

  • A safe place for people to gather and support one another.
  • A source of clear, current information.
  • A place to match local needs with local offers of help.
  • A link that passes serious needs up to the ECC/EOC.
A hub helps a community help itself first.
02

Choosing and setting up the space

Picking a venue and making it work.

 OPEN / CLOSE

A good hub venue is known, accessible, and safe. Set it up so people can find their way and the team can work.

  • Pick a building the community already knows — a hall, marae, or school.
  • Check it's structurally safe before opening.
  • Set up zones: reception, information board, a quiet/welfare area.
  • Make signage clear so arrivals know where to go.
Activity

Scout a venue

Identify one or two buildings in your community that could serve as a hub. Note access, parking, and any safety concerns.

03

Opening the hub

The first actions when you activate.

 OPEN / CLOSE

When you decide to open, work through a simple startup sequence so nothing essential is missed.

  • Confirm the building is safe to use.
  • Set up the situation board and public information board.
  • Assign the first roles — supervisor, information, reception.
  • Open reception so arrivals are greeted and directed.
  • Start a log of decisions and events.
Open in a calm, orderly way — first impressions set the tone for everyone who arrives.
04

Running day-to-day

Keeping the hub functioning and sustainable.

 OPEN / CLOSE

Once open, a hub needs rhythm: regular check-ins, fresh information, and care for the volunteers running it.

  • Hold short, regular team huddles to align priorities.
  • Keep information boards current and mark anything unverified.
  • Rotate volunteers and prompt breaks, food, and water.
  • Know when to scale down overnight or stand down.
Activity

Plan a shift

Sketch a simple roster for a 12-hour hub day — who's on, when they break, and how handovers work.
