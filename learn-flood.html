Sandbagging & Flood Response

Protect people and property from flooding — effective sandbagging, safe water management, and knowing when to stop and get clear.

Draft content. This course is a practical starting point. Please review it and have someone with relevant expertise check anything safety-critical before relying on it.

01

Understanding flood risk

How floods threaten people and property.

 OPEN / CLOSE

Floods are one of New Zealand's most common hazards. Understanding how water moves and rises helps you protect people and property — and know when to stop and get clear.

Never underestimate moving water. Even shallow flowing water can knock you off your feet.
02

Sandbagging basics

Building an effective barrier.

 OPEN / CLOSE

Sandbags slow and divert water. Filled and laid correctly, they protect doorways and low points; done poorly, they offer little.

  • Fill bags about half to two-thirds full — not packed tight.
  • Lay them like brickwork, overlapping and tamped down.
  • Fold the open end under, facing away from the water flow.
  • Build in layers, staggering the joints between rows.
  • Protect doorways, airbricks, and low entry points first.
Activity

Plan your barrier

Identify where water would enter your building first. That's where your bags go.

03

Pumps and water management

Moving water away safely.

 OPEN / CLOSE

Pumps help clear water that gets through, but must be used safely — especially around electricity.

  • Keep electrical equipment and cords out of water.
  • Direct pumped water well away from buildings.
  • Never work alone in rising water.
  • Know when to stop — property is replaceable, people aren't.
If water is rising faster than you can manage it, stop and get to safety.
