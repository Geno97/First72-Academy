Civil Defence Emergency Hub

01

What a Civil Defence hub is

How community hubs connect with CDEM.

 OPEN / CLOSE

A Civil Defence Emergency Hub is a community hub that connects into the official Civil Defence Emergency Management (CDEM) system, coordinating with councils and the wider response.

This course gives a general overview. Your local CDEM group's official guidance and training should be followed for any formal hub role.

A CD hub bridges community effort and the official response.
02

Setting up and integrating

Connecting your hub to the wider system.

 OPEN / CLOSE

A CD hub works best when it plugs cleanly into the official structure — knowing who to report to and how information flows up and down.

  • Establish the hub using the community hub model.
  • Know your local CDEM contacts and reporting lines.
  • Pass verified information and serious needs to the ECC/EOC.
  • Receive and share official information with the community.
03

Working with councils

Coordinating with official agencies.

 OPEN / CLOSE

Councils and CDEM groups coordinate the wider response. Your hub supports that, not replaces it.

  • Follow official guidance and priorities.
  • Keep your reporting clear and timely.
  • Use common language so information isn't lost.
  • Support — don't duplicate or work around — the official response.
Community hubs and councils work best as partners, pulling in the same direction.
