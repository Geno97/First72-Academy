Team Leadership in CDEM

Lead a civil defence team well — making sound decisions under uncertainty, sustaining people and resources, and staying calm under pressure.

Draft content. This course is a practical starting point. Please review it and have someone with relevant expertise check anything safety-critical before relying on it.

Click any module to expand it. Each one has a short lesson, and most include an activity or a quick knowledge check. Work at your own pace.

01

Leading under pressure

What CDEM team leadership asks of you.

 OPEN / CLOSE

Leading a team in civil defence means making decisions with incomplete information, keeping people safe, and staying calm so others can too.

Your calm is contagious. So is your panic. Lead the tone you want.
02

Decision-making under uncertainty

Acting well when you don't have all the facts.

 OPEN / CLOSE

You'll rarely have complete information. Good leaders make the best decision they can on what's verified, then keep reassessing.

  • Separate what's confirmed from what's rumour.
  • Make a clear, timely decision rather than freezing.
  • Communicate the decision and the reasoning.
  • Reassess as new information arrives.
Activity

Decide and revisit

Recall a decision you made with limited information. What would have helped you decide faster and revisit it sooner?

03

Logistics and sustainability

Keeping people and resources going.

 OPEN / CLOSE

Responses can run for days. Leaders manage not just tasks but the people and supplies that sustain the effort.

  • Plan rotations so the team doesn't burn out.
  • Track resources and reorder before you run short.
  • Build in rest, food, and water as non-negotiables.
  • Hand over clearly between shifts.
