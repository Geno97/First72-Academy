Road Closure & Traffic Management

An awareness-level introduction to road closures, cordons, and supporting responders safely. Accredited training is required for operational roles.

Draft content. This course is a practical starting point. Please review it and have someone with relevant expertise check anything safety-critical before relying on it.

01

Why this matters

Keeping people safe around hazards and traffic.

 OPEN / CLOSE

After an emergency, roads may be dangerous, and people need to be kept away from hazards. Helping manage traffic and cordons supports responders — but it carries real risk and requires proper training.

Important

This is awareness-level content only

This course gives general awareness, not certification. For a recognised qualification or any hands-on practice, you must complete accredited training with a qualified instructor and the official course materials. Do not rely on this course alone for safety-critical or certified work.

Working near traffic and hazards is dangerous. Training and the right equipment are not optional.
02

Cordons and keeping people safe

The purpose of closures and cordons.

 OPEN / CLOSE

Cordons keep people away from danger and let responders work. Understanding their purpose helps you support them safely.

  • Cordons protect the public and create safe working space.
  • Always follow the directions of emergency services.
  • Make yourself visible — hi-vis and safe positioning.
  • Never put yourself between moving vehicles and a hazard.
03

Supporting responders

Helping safely within your limits.

 OPEN / CLOSE

Your role is to support, not to take on tasks beyond your training.

  • Direct people calmly and clearly.
  • Report hazards to the responders in charge.
  • Stay within tasks you're trained and equipped for.
  • Step back if a situation becomes unsafe.
If it isn't safe, don't do it. No task near traffic is worth your life.
