Coordinated Incident Management (CIMS)

A plain-language introduction to New Zealand's CIMS framework — the common roles and language that let agencies and communities respond together.

01

What CIMS is

New Zealand's framework for coordinating response.

 OPEN / CLOSE

The Coordinated Incident Management System (CIMS) is the framework New Zealand uses so that different agencies and teams can work together during an emergency, using common roles and language.

CIMS exists so everyone responding speaks the same language and knows how they fit together.

This course is a plain-language introduction. Official CIMS training and documentation should be used for any formal or operational role.

02

The core functions

How response work is organised.

 OPEN / CLOSE

CIMS organises response work into common functions so responsibilities are clear. At a high level these include control (overall coordination), and supporting functions such as planning/intelligence, operations, logistics, and public information.

  • Control — overall coordination of the response.
  • Operations — the hands-on response activity.
  • Planning / Intelligence — building the picture and planning ahead.
  • Logistics — people, equipment, and supplies.
  • Public Information Management — keeping people informed.
03

Why it matters for communities

How local effort connects to the bigger response.

 OPEN / CLOSE

Even at community level, understanding CIMS helps your hub connect cleanly into the wider response — knowing who to report to and how information flows.

Local effort works best when it plugs neatly into the official response, not around it.
